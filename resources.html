What Is a Tradeline?

A tradeline is any account that appears on your credit report. Every credit card, mortgage, auto loan, and personal line of credit is a tradeline. Together, they tell the story of your relationship with credit.

Credit bureaus — Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion — collect and store information about these tradelines, including payment history, credit limit, age of the account, and current balance. This data feeds directly into your FICO score.

What Is an Authorized User Tradeline?

When a credit card holder adds someone as an authorized user on their account, that account's history appears on the authorized user's credit report. This is a fully legal and widely used credit-building strategy.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take for a tradeline to post? ▾
Tradelines post during the reporting period shown on the listing — this is the window when the card issuer sends updated information to the credit bureaus. Most tradelines post within 30–45 days of purchase. You'll see the tradeline appear on your credit report 1–3 days after the reporting date.
Do I get access to the actual credit card? ▾
No. As an authorized user, you appear on the account — but you don't receive a physical card, and you have no ability to make charges on it. This is by design. The only purpose of the arrangement is to have the account history appear on your credit report.
What payment methods do you accept? ▾
We accept cryptocurrency (Bitcoin, Ethereum, USDT, Solana) and TradeLine Marketplace Store Credit. We do not accept credit cards, ACH checks, PayPal, or Venmo. Crypto payments keep fees low and require no identity documents.
Is there a minimum order? ▾
No minimum order. You can purchase a single tradeline if that's all you need. For buyers purchasing 2 or more, apply the coupon code PKGDEAL at checkout for a discount.
Can I purchase tradelines for someone else? ▾
Yes. During the checkout process you'll enter the Authorized User's personal details (name, DOB, SSN last 4, address). The tradeline posts to that person's credit report. Brokers and credit consultants regularly purchase tradelines on behalf of their clients.
Do you sell tradelines in all states? ▾
We sell tradelines in all U.S. states except Georgia. If your billing address or the AU's address is in Georgia, we're unable to process your order.
How long does the authorized user stay on the account? ▾
The AU remains active for the reporting period shown on the listing (typically 2–3 months). After the period ends, the card holder removes the AU from the account. Once removed, the tradeline may or may not stay on your credit report — this depends on the bureau and the card issuer.
Can using tradelines hurt my credit? ▾
It's extremely unlikely. Adding a positive, aged tradeline with low utilization and perfect payment history should only help your profile. However, if the card holder on the tradeline you purchased later misses a payment or has the card closed, it could affect the tradeline — which is why we guarantee our tradeline holders meet quality standards.
