What Is a Tradeline?

A tradeline is any account that appears on your credit report. Every credit card, mortgage, auto loan, and personal line of credit is a tradeline. Together, they tell the story of your relationship with credit.

Credit bureaus — Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion — collect and store information about these tradelines, including payment history, credit limit, age of the account, and current balance. This data feeds directly into your FICO score.

What Is an Authorized User Tradeline?

When a credit card holder adds someone as an authorized user on their account, that account's history appears on the authorized user's credit report. This is a fully legal, widely-used credit-building strategy that has existed for decades.

Banks like Chase, American Express, Discover, and Bank of America all report authorized user accounts to the major credit bureaus. This practice is so common that roughly one-third of all U.S. credit profiles already contain at least one authorized user tradeline.

✅ Why It's Legal

Adding authorized users is explicitly permitted under the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA), which was specifically amended in 1988 to require bureaus to consider authorized user accounts when scoring. This is the same mechanism parents use to help children build credit.

How Does It Affect My Credit Score?

When you become an authorized user on an aged account with a high credit limit, low utilization, and perfect payment history, several key FICO factors can improve:

  • Age of credit history — The account's full age is added to your report, potentially raising your average account age.
  • Credit utilization — A high-limit card with low utilization lowers your overall utilization ratio.
  • Number of accounts — Adding a positive account improves your credit mix and depth of file.
  • Payment history — The primary cardholder's perfect payment history is reflected on your report.

Who Should Buy a Tradeline?

Tradelines are typically purchased by individuals who:

  • Have a thin credit file (few or no accounts) and need to establish a credit history quickly
  • Are preparing for a mortgage application in the next 30–90 days
  • Want to lower their credit utilization ratio
  • Are rebuilding credit after a bankruptcy or derogatory mark
  • Need to qualify for a business loan that requires a strong personal credit profile
⚠ What Tradelines Cannot Do

Tradelines do not remove negative items (late payments, collections, bankruptcies) from your credit report. They work alongside — not instead of — proper credit repair strategies. We recommend consulting with a credit professional to determine whether tradelines are the right tool for your specific situation.

Tradeline Buyer's Guide

Purchasing the right tradeline requires matching the card's characteristics to your specific credit goals. Not all tradelines are equal — and buying the wrong one can mean spending money without seeing meaningful results.

Factor 1: Card Age

Card age is one of the most impactful factors. FICO's algorithm weighs both the age of your oldest account and the average age of all accounts. Adding an older card raises both numbers.

  • Under 5 years: Minimal impact on age-related scores. Good for adding depth to an already-aged file.
  • 5–10 years: Solid choice for most buyers with thin files.
  • 10–15 years: Strong impact. Recommended for pre-mortgage applications.
  • 15+ years: Maximum impact on credit age. Premium-priced, but worth it for critical applications.

Factor 2: Credit Limit

Higher credit limits reduce your overall utilization ratio. If you carry any balances on other cards, a $30,000 limit tradeline will have a much larger positive impact on utilization than a $5,000 one.

Factor 3: Utilization on the Tradeline

All tradelines listed on TradeLine Marketplace maintain utilization below 15%. Lower utilization = better impact. We verify this before listing.

Factor 4: Bank

Most major banks report authorized users to all 3 bureaus. Chase, Discover, American Express, Barclays, and Bank of America are among the most reliable reporters. We verify each tradeline's reporting history before listing.

How Many Tradelines Should I Buy?

This depends on your goal and current profile:

  • Thin file (0–2 accounts): 2–3 tradelines recommended — one aged, one with a high limit.
  • Established file with score issues: 1–2 targeted tradelines. Focus on high limit or high age, depending on your score breakdown.
  • Pre-mortgage in 60 days: 1–2 tradelines at least 45 days before your application date. Confirm the reporting window aligns with your timeline.
💡 Pro Tip: Check the Reporting Window

Each tradeline has a specific "buy by" deadline and a reporting window. If you need the tradeline to post before a specific date (like a loan application), make sure the reporting period ends before that date. Use our Tradeline Calculator to map timelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take for a tradeline to post? ▾
Tradelines post during the reporting period shown on the listing — the window when the card issuer sends updated information to the credit bureaus. Most tradelines post within 30–45 days of purchase. You'll see the tradeline appear 1–3 days after the reporting date passes.
Do I get access to the actual credit card? ▾
No. As an authorized user, you appear on the account but receive no physical card and have no ability to make charges. The only purpose of the arrangement is for the account history to appear on your credit report.
What payment methods do you accept? ▾
We accept cryptocurrency: USDT (TRC-20, ERC-20, BEP-20, Solana networks), Bitcoin (BEP-20, Bitcoin network, RENEC), and Litecoin. We also accept TradeLine Marketplace Store Credit. We do not accept credit cards, ACH, PayPal, or Venmo.
Is there a minimum order? ▾
No minimum order. You can purchase a single tradeline. For 2 or more tradelines, apply coupon code PKGDEAL at checkout for a package discount.
Can I purchase tradelines for someone else (a client)? ▾
Yes. During checkout you'll enter the authorized user's personal details — name, DOB, ID information, and SSN last 4. The tradeline posts to that person's credit report. Brokers and credit consultants do this regularly.
Do you sell tradelines in all states? ▾
We sell tradelines in all U.S. states except Georgia. If your billing address or the authorized user's address is in Georgia, we're unable to process your order per state regulations.
How long does the authorized user stay on the account? ▾
The AU remains active for the reporting period shown on the listing (typically 2–3 months). After the period ends, the card holder removes the AU. Once removed, the tradeline may or may not stay on your credit report depending on the bureau and card issuer.
Can using tradelines hurt my credit? ▾
It's extremely unlikely. Adding a positive, aged tradeline with low utilization and perfect payment history should only help your profile. We screen all tradeline holders to ensure accounts meet quality standards before listing.
How do I verify the tradeline posted to my credit report? ▾
Check your credit reports at AnnualCreditReport.com or use a credit monitoring service like Credit Karma, Experian, or MyFICO. Look for the card issuer's name and the authorized user notation. Allow 3–7 days after the reporting window closes.
What if the tradeline doesn't post? ▾
Submit a Non-Posting Report within 7 days of the reporting period ending. Our team will investigate and issue a full store credit within 3 business days if confirmed.
Is it legal to buy tradelines? ▾
Yes. Adding authorized users to credit card accounts is legal and explicitly permitted under the Equal Credit Opportunity Act. The practice is used by millions of families (parents adding children) and is recognized by FICO's scoring models. Always consult a financial advisor about your specific situation.
